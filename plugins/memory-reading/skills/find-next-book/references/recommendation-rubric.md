# Recommendation Rubric

Use this rubric to rank candidates consistently. Score privately; do not show numbers unless the user asks.

## Eligibility gates

Remove a candidate before scoring when any of these is true:

- The title, author, or core premise cannot be verified.
- The recommendation relies mainly on an unrelated or sensitive memory.
- The book substantially repeats something the user has already mastered or completed, unless rereading is the explicit goal.
- The source material is too weak to explain why the book fits.

## Score out of 100

| Dimension | Weight | Question |
|---|---:|---|
| Current goal fit | 30 | Does this directly address the user's current problem or desired outcome? |
| Why-now timing | 20 | Is this useful at the user's present stage rather than merely relevant in general? |
| Knowledge-gap fit | 15 | Does it add a missing model, method, or perspective without excessive duplication? |
| Actionability | 15 | Can the user apply, test, or discuss something meaningful after reading it? |
| Constraint fit | 10 | Does difficulty, length, format, language, and pace match the user? |
| Evidence confidence | 10 | Are the book facts and recommendation rationale supported by reliable, current evidence? |

## Penalties

- Subtract 15 for relying on memory that may be stale and cannot be reconciled with current context.
- Subtract 15 for strong overlap with books or concepts the user already knows.
- Subtract 10 for a clear length, difficulty, language, or format mismatch.
- Subtract 10 when availability in the user's requested catalog cannot be confirmed.

## Decision thresholds

- 80–100: strong primary recommendation.
- 65–79: reasonable recommendation; state the main uncertainty.
- Below 65: do not present as a confident primary choice. Ask one discriminating question or give a clearly tentative option.

## Alternative selection

Return alternatives only when they express a real tradeoff:

- shorter and more approachable;
- deeper and more systematic;
- practical and immediately applicable;
- exploratory and outside the user's usual pattern.

Do not return several books that serve the same purpose.
